Recognizing the Warning Signs of a Termite Infestation

Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can cause significant damage to a property without immediate visible signs. However, being aware of the potential warning signs can help you detect an infestation early and minimize the extent of the damage. Early detection is key to effective and less costly termite control. If you notice any of the following signs at your Mattydale property, it's crucial to contact a professional termite exterminator like Syracuse Termite immediately for an inspection.

Common Indicators of Termite Activity:

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls:

Subterranean termites, the most common type in Mattydale, build mud tubes or 'shelter tubes' from soil and wood cellulose. These tubes, about the width of a pencil, serve as protected pathways for termites to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home's wooden structure) while protecting them from predators and dry air. Finding mud tubes on your foundation, walls, or other surfaces is a clear indication of active subterranean termites near or within your property. Don't disturb these tubes before an inspection, as it can make it harder for the exterminator to locate the active colony.

Winged Swarmers or Discarded Wings:

Termite colonies reproduce by sending out 'swarmers' – winged termites that fly from an existing colony to establish new ones. Swarming typically occurs in the spring or after a rain shower. If you see winged insects resembling flying ants indoors, especially near windows or light sources, or find discarded wings near windowsills, doorways, or spiderwebs, it's a strong signal of a nearby termite colony. These swarmers are attempting to find a mate and start a new colony, potentially in your home.

Damaged Wood:

Termites literally eat away at the wooden structure of your home or business. While visible damage might not be apparent on the surface initially, as the infestation progresses, you may notice signs of damaged wood. This can manifest as wood that appears darker, blistered, or even has a honeycombed pattern when broken. Tapping on suspected areas of wood can help, as termite-damaged wood often sounds hollow or dull due to the internal tunneling.

Frass (Termite Droppings):

Drywood termites, while less common than subterranean termites in Mattydale, expel their droppings, known as 'frass,' from small kick-out holes in infested wood. Frass resembles small, often hexagonal, pellets that can accumulate in small piles near infested areas. If you find small piles of what looks like sawdust or pepper near wooden furniture, door frames, or other wooden structures, it could be a sign of a drywood termite infestation.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings:

In advanced stages of a termite infestation, significant structural damage can occur. As termites tunnel through and consume the wood within your floors and ceilings, these structures can weaken and begin to sag. While other factors can cause sagging, if accompanied by other termite signs, it's a serious indicator of extensive damage requiring immediate professional attention.

Stuck Windows or Doors:

As termites consume wood, they can introduce moisture and cause the wood to warp. This warping can lead to windows and doors becoming difficult to open or close. While humidity and other factors can cause this, if it occurs suddenly or in conjunction with other potential termite indicators, it warrants investigation.

Clicking Sounds Within Walls:

Although less common for homeowners to hear, soldier termites within a colony will sometimes bang their heads against the wood or shake their bodies when the colony is disturbed. This creates a faint clicking sound that can occasionally be heard from within infested walls.

If you observe any of these signs, it's not the time to panic, but it is the time to act. Contacting Syracuse Termite for a professional inspection is the most important step you can take to assess the situation accurately and implement an effective termite control strategy. Ignoring these signs can lead to significant and costly structural repairs down the line. Our experienced technicians can confirm the presence of termites, identify the source of the infestation, and recommend the best course of action to protect your property.
